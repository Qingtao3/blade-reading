# Example Prompts

Use these prompts to trigger `blade-reading`.

## 1. Personal 30-day plan

```text
I am a first-year university student. I want to improve judgment, business understanding, and content creation. No motivational fluff, no pile of famous classics, and no books that look impressive but are unrealistic for my current stage. Build me a 30-day blade-reading plan.
```

## 2. Candidate list triage

```text
Filter this candidate list: Principles, The Almanack of Naval Ravikant, Poor Charlie's Almanack, Shoe Dog, Steve Jobs, The Snowball, The Making of the Modern Chinese State, Principles of Economics, Thinking, Fast and Slow, Deep Work, Peak, Courage to Be Disliked, To Live, The Stranger, Siddhartha, Meditations, Influence, Amusing Ourselves to Death, The Pyramid Principle.

Tell me which deserve deep reading, which should be normal reads, which are enough to summarize with AI, and which are not a priority.
```

## 3. Business books without hype

```text
I want business books, but not wealth mindset, business cognition, or success formulas. Filter books that help me understand real transactions, cash flow, organizations, risk, games, and execution. Explain each book's irreducible value.
```

## 4. AI tools and creator workflow

```text
I want to learn AI tools, prompting, video editing, and creator operations. Help me decide which parts should be solved through AI plus practice, and which books have long-term reading value.
```

## 5. Shelf cleanup

```text
Here is my reading queue. Organize it into main categories and secondary tags, and tell me which books are not a priority:

To Live, One Hundred Years of Solitude, The Brothers Karamazov, Principles, From Zero to One, The Innovator's Dilemma, Deng Xiaoping and the Transformation of China, From the Soil, The Psychology of Money, Automate the Boring Stuff with Python.
```
