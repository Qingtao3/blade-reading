## 2. User problem model

The user's problem is not a lack of books. The real need is a stricter filtering mechanism: use limited reading time to train judgment, reality sense, business understanding, and expression. The filtering therefore prioritizes books that show complex choices, real constraints, and action-convertible structures.

## 4. Key recommended books

### Shoe Dog -- Phil Knight

- Type: biography / business biography
- Score: 86
- Recommendation level: S
- Reading mode: normal full read
- Main category: Biography: choices in the real world
- Secondary tags: business and wealth; action conversion; real-world complexity
- Priority: 1
- Why: This is not a slogan book about entrepreneurship. It shows how a person keeps making choices under cash-flow pressure, supply-chain risk, organizational disorder, and personal limitations.
- Real problem solved: Understand business as cash flow, execution, risk, and long-term choices, not as idea competition.
- Irreducible value: AI can summarize Nike's history, but it cannot replace following the author's uncertainty, risk, mistakes, and costs across the journey.
- What AI can replace: company timeline, relationship map, key event overview.
- What must be read personally: early cash-flow pressure, key negotiations, loss of control during growth, and the cost behind success.
- Reading focus: Watch the constraints behind each key choice, not the conclusion that looks correct in hindsight.
- Possible trap: Reading it as a heroic startup story and ignoring luck, risk, and cost.

### The Making of the Modern Chinese State -- Lan Xiaohuan

- Type: framework calibration / real-world understanding
- Score: 88
- Recommendation level: S
- Reading mode: deep read
- Main category: Business and wealth: cash flow, organization, risk
- Secondary tags: institutions; economic incentives; China reality
- Priority: 2
- Why: It turns macroeconomics, government behavior, and local development from abstract words into concrete mechanisms.
- Real problem solved: Build respect for reality by understanding constraints between individuals, organizations, and institutions.
- Irreducible value: Its value is in structure and mechanisms, not isolated claims.
- What AI can replace: concept explanations and chapter summaries.
- What must be read personally: how finance, land, and local government incentives form an explanatory framework.
- Reading focus: Do not memorize conclusions; watch how variables affect one another.
- Possible trap: Treating it as a simple China-economy primer instead of using it to train structural reality sense.

## 5. AI summary only / selective reading

- Deep Work: Its core methods can be compressed into environment design, reducing context switching, and fixed deep-work blocks. Ask AI to turn it into a personal execution checklist.
- Automate the Boring Stuff with Python: A tool-operation book. Query it only around concrete automation tasks.
- The Psychology of Money: Useful, but many chapters reinforce the same core ideas. Select chapters.

## 6. Not a priority

- The Brothers Karamazov: Its irreducible value is high, but it is too heavy for this 30-day goal. Save it for a more stable reading period.
- Principles: Valuable, but easy to read as management quotes. Without organizational practice, current conversion is limited.

## 7. 30-day reading order

1. Days 1-10: Shoe Dog, normal full read  
   Goal: understand business choices, cash-flow pressure, and uncertainty in action.

2. Days 11-22: The Making of the Modern Chinese State, deep read  
   Goal: build real-world structural sense and understand institutions and incentives.

3. Days 23-30: To Live, slow read  
   Goal: train human understanding, fate sense, and sensitivity; prevent growth narratives from becoming too utilitarian.

AI-summary helpers:

- Deep Work: ask AI for 7 executable attention rules.
- Automate the Boring Stuff with Python: query only when doing a concrete task.
